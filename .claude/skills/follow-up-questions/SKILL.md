# Intake Follow-Up Questions

When a recruiter gives you a JD or role to source for, ALWAYS ask these questions before sourcing. Don't ask all at once -- group into 2-3 natural batches.

## Batch 1: Must-Haves (Ask First)

- **Must-have skills:** "Any skills that are absolute dealbreakers if missing?"
- **Experience level:** "What's the minimum years of experience? Is there a max?"
- **Location:** "Remote OK? Hybrid? On-site only? Any geo restrictions?"
- **Candidate count:** "How many candidates do you want me to find? (I recommend 15-20 for a good pipeline)"

## Batch 2: Nice-to-Haves (Ask After Batch 1)

- **Company preferences:** "Any specific companies you'd love to poach from? Any to avoid?"
- **Salary range:** "What's the comp range? (Helps me filter realistically)"
- **Outreach threshold:** "What minimum score should I outreach? (I suggest 7+/10)"
- **Timeline:** "How urgent is this? Affects how aggressively we source."

## Batch 3: Style (Ask Only If Recruiter Is Engaged)

- **Outreach tone:** "Any specific tone for emails? More casual or more formal?"
- **Screening process:** "What happens after a candidate replies? Call? Take-home? Direct to HM?"
- **Special context:** "Anything else I should know -- recent layoffs at the company, competing offers, team dynamics?"

## Rules

- Never source without at least Batch 1 answered
- If recruiter says "just go" or "use your judgment," use reasonable defaults and state your assumptions
- Save ALL preferences to Graphiti so future searches for this role/company are informed
- If this is a repeat role for the same company, search Graphiti first and say "Last time you wanted X, Y, Z -- still the same?"
