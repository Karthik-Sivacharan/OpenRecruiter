# OpenRecruiter Product Context

## What This Is
An autonomous AI recruiting agency that runs the full pipeline: sourcing, enrichment, analysis, scoring, personalized outreach, and drip campaigns. The recruiter interacts via a chat interface, approves key decisions, and the agent handles everything else.

## Users
- **Primary:** Technical recruiters and founders hiring engineers. They know what good candidates look like but don't have time to source, research, and email 50 people.
- **Secondary:** Candidates receiving outreach. They should feel like a human who genuinely studied their work reached out -- not a mass email blast.

## Brand Voice
- Warm, direct, no corporate fluff
- Confident but not arrogant
- Show the work: "I found Jane's distributed training project on GitHub" not "I identified a strong candidate"
- Recruiter-facing: conversational, efficient, numbers-driven
- Candidate-facing: personalized, specific, respectful of their time

## Register: Product
Design serves the product, not IS the product. Prioritize clarity, speed, and information density over visual flair. System fonts are fine. The chat interface should feel like a smart assistant, not a design showcase.

## Anti-References (What We Do NOT Look Like)
- Generic SaaS dashboards with purple gradients and glassmorphism
- LinkedIn recruiter spam ("I came across your profile and was impressed...")
- Over-designed chat UIs with excessive animations
- Corporate recruiting software with 50 fields per candidate

## Strategic Principles
- Autonomous but human-approved: agent runs the pipeline, recruiter approves spending and sending
- Transparent pipeline: recruiter can always see what the agent did and why
- Credit-aware: always show costs before spending API credits
- Quality over quantity: 10 deeply researched candidates beat 100 shallow ones
